Seminar 1: STRIDE and DREAD tools

Preparation

For this seminar we were to use Microsofts STRIDE and DREAD tools to perform a risk assessment based on our disccuion 1 around Glisson et al (2015) Compromising a Medical Mannequin and discuss what the highest risks were and what assumptions had been made. We prepared 2 slides to cover a risk assessment using DREAD as an analysis tool.

Identified Threats

Using the STRIDE threat model we have identified 2 main threats to the iStan devices

1. Threat of denial of service on the iStan devices

As proven by the case study and discussion posts it is possible to cause a denial of service attack on the network making the iStan devices and software unavailable.

2. Threat of tampering with the devices following a brute force attack

Due to the ability to brute force attack the PIN on the network router this could allow a malicious actor to tamper with the iStan devices and any connected information.

Threat of Denial of Service

Damage - Would stop the use of the devices while under attack = 2

Reproducibility - Easy to launch the attack using open source tools = 3

Exploitability - Little to no pen test and computer science skills required to create the attack = 2

Affected Users - Will only affect devices in the specific location = 1

Discoverability - Easy to discover the vulnerability as was done in the case study = 2

Total risk score = 10

Threat of Tampering

Damage - Would affect the training of medical professionals = 3

Reproducibility - Easy to launch the attack using open source tools = 3

Exploitability - Must be in within a certain distance to the devices = 1

Affected Users - Could impact many patients by medical professionals with insufficient training = 3

Discoverability - Easy to discover the vulnerability as was done in the case study = 2

Total risk score = 12
