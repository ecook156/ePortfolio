Seminar 4: Security Standards

Preparation

For this seminar we were to review security standards and exmplain which of these were applicable to our assigned website and how they can be applied to the site and ensure complaince with the relevant standards. We discussed the questions as a group and prepared responses using a shared google document.

Which of the standards discussed in the sources above would apply to the website/ organisation assigned to you for the assessment?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is applicable due to the site providing services to anyone living in europe.

PCI DSS is not applicable as there is no payment or ecommerce done through the site.

HIPAA is not applicable as there is no personal health information stored required.

Evaluate your assigned website against the appropriate standards and decide how you would check if standards were being met?

We based our review around the 7 principles of GDPR and assessed our website against each of the principles

Article 5(1)a - Lawfulness, fairness and transparency - “Data shall be processed lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner in relation to individuals”

Users of allmytype must understand how their email address and name will be used

Users should be given the option to subscribe or opt out

Consent notice could be included

Article 5(1)b - Purpose limitation - “Data shall be collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes; further processing for archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research purposes or statistical purposes shall not be considered to be incompatible with the initial purposes”

Their email addresses used for subscription can only be used for that purpose and should not be used to contact them about anything else

Can’t be used for marketing purposes unless specified

Article 5(1)c - Data minimisation - “Data shall be adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed”

Only names and email addresses should be kept as that is all that is needed

Article 5(1)d - Accuracy - “Data shall be accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date; every reasonable step must be taken to ensure that personal data that are inaccurate, having regard to the purposes for which they are processed, are erased or rectified without delay”

The data stored must be accurate with the date that is submitted

Prevent tampering from being possible

Article 5(1)e - Storage Limitation - “Data shall be kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed; personal data may be stored for longer periods insofar as the personal data will be processed solely for archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research purposes or statistical purposes subject to implementation of the appropriate technical and organisational measures required by the GDPR in order to safeguard the rights and freedoms of individuals”

There must be defined retention periods in place suitable for the use of the site

After the retention period is reached, the email addresses should be erased or when requested by the customer

Article 5(1)f - Integrity and confidentiality (security) - “Data shall be processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data, including protection against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction or damage, using appropriate technical or organisational measures”

Security controls must be put in place to protect the email addresses stored

Article 5(1)g - Accountability - “Data shall be The controller shall be responsible for, and be able to demonstrate compliance with, paragraph 1”

The owner of allmytype.co.uk is responsible for the data submitted to the site and therefore accountable for any breaches

What would your recommendations be to meet those standards?

Provide a link for users to read the privacy statement and to decide if they agree or consent to these statements

Create a link that is clear and visible for users to select to opt out of subscription for every user engagement

Appoint a dedicated data protection officer

Documentation of every process taken to monitor compliance and process that should be followed to report possible breach in a timely manner

All forms of breaches whether reported or not should be documented

Principles like check twice, send once should be used to improve accuracy

Regular supervision and support

Create information walls and implement security controls to restrict access and auditing systems

Check existing practise against the expectations of the GDPR to evaluate the level of compliance

Perform regular management engagement on data privacy awareness

Regular staff engagements and training on how to protect personal data and how to determine if a breach has occurred. Mandatory data protection induction for all staff

Disable technologies like autofill

Links to privacy statements should be sent to persons whose personal details have been collected

Perform regular self assessment to measure how the principle has been adhered to

What assumptions have you made?

User sensitive information will be collected and stored in a database

The website is available to the public

The website is owned by an organisation with employees
