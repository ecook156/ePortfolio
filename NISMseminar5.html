Seminar 5: Data Breach Case Study

Equifax Data Breach - May 2017

For this seminar we were to read Read Swinhoe, D., 2020. The 15 Biggest Data Breaches Of The 21St Century. [online] CSO Online. We then selected a case study from the options and reviewed the data breach that occured by asnwering some questions for discussion.

Summary

Equifax was using an open-source software (Apache Struts) to help employees and consumers manage credit disputes.

Apache released a major update for the Struts program in March, 2017 due to a security flaw in the software.

They urged IT managers to update immediately to patch the flaw but Equifax did not deploy the latest update and was therefore left vulnerable.

The breach wasn't discovered until July 2017 and had lasted for 76 days before Equifax patched the exploit.

It was also discovered that the personal information held was not encrypted.

What types of data were affected?

Criminals first targeted the login credentials of Equifax employees.

This allowed them to search credit reports and other consumer data as authorised users.

The cybercriminals ran a search of more than 9000 consumer records.

Most of the information compromised was first and last names, social security numbers, addresses, and driver’s license numbers.

The exposure of all of this data could lead to identity and credit card fraud.

Were any escalation(s) stopped - how?

The result of the Equifax breach was mainly due to not maintaining and updating their systems and applications with the latest updates and security patches.

Were the ICO notified?

The ICO were notified and issued a penalty of £500,000 under section 55a of the Data Protection Act 1998

The commissioner found that Equifax did not take appropriate technical and organisational measures to prevent the unlawful processing of data (ICO, 2017)

As this breach pre dated GDPR the fine was less that it would have been if it happened today.

Was the Business Continuity Plan instigated?

No - part of the cause of the breach was that Equifax had a lack of breach detection protocols.

Were affected individuals notified?

Equifax went public about the breach in September 2017 - it's not clear how and when customer affected were notified

If you had been the ISM for the organisation you selected, what mitigations would you have put in place to stop any recurrences?

Ensure there was an appropriate patch management and upgrade process in place to maintain all of the systems and deploy any updates as soon as possible.

Role based access control for personal information could have helped deter the attackers or made it more difficult for them to exploit the information.

The personal data stored should always be encrypted, so it there is a breach, it is unlikely to be deciphered.

All experian employees should receive at least annual security and data protection training to enforce why these actions are important.
